== Collecting Training Data

@n Evaluate `d = train(10)` to to run the simulation at 15 frames per second, and *save your training data* to the variable `d`. What do you get back when you evaluate `d`? @fitb{}{@ifsoln{my driving data}}

@n What happens when you finish a track twice? @fitb{}{@ifsoln{It generates a different track!}}

@n Want to try again at a faster speed? Evaluate `d2 = train(20)`.

@n *Use the driving simulator a few times, until you can get complete 2-3 different tracks*

@n Click "Run" and create two datasets: `good` and `bad`. Make `good` your most successful driving record, and then make a `bad` dataset on purpose where you crash into walls.

== Training and Using the Model

@n Train a function using `mr-code(good, [list: "speed", "curve-sharpness", "offset"], "steering-angle")`, and define that function as `good-predictor`. Do the same with `bad-predictor`.

@n Run the simulation using your two functions: `drive(20, good-predictor)`, `drive(20, bad-predictor)`. What happens?

@n There's one more column in your dataset (`heading-error`), which records how far out-of-alignment the vehicle is with the road it's own. Try training a new model, including this attribute in as another predictor.
