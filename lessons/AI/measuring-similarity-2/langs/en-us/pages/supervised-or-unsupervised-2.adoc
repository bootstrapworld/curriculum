Machine learning models are all around us, using a mix of different learning methods. Can you tell the difference between @vocab{supervised learning} and @vocab{unsupervised learning}? Supervised methods learn from labeled examples — sorting things into categories (_classification_), predicting a number (_regression_), or recommending by similarity to examples you've tagged (building a _centroid_). Unsupervised methods hunt for patterns with no labels at all.
