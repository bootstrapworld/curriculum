== Combining Functions

You can also combine these functions to produce alternatives that are more than 1 edit from the original.

@n In your own words, what do you think `swaps(swaps("hello"))` will do? @fitb{}{}
@blanklines{2}{@ifsoln{it will compute all possible swaps for "hello", then compute every swap for _those_ swaps!}}

@n In your own words, what do you think `swaps(subs("hello"))` will do? @fitb{}{}
@blanklines{2}{@ifsoln{For every possible 1-letter substitution, find every possible swap}}

== Removing "Junk" Words

@n Evaluate `only-real(subs("hello"), WORDS-L)`. What does `only-real` do? @fitb{}{@ifsoln{It removes any word that isn't in the dictionary}}
@fitb{}{}

@n Try other dictionaries. For example, Evaluate `only-real(subs("hello"), WORDS-M)`. Why do we get different results? +
@fitb{}{The size of the dictionary determines what words the algorithm will return. Smaller dictionaries mean more "unknown words."}

== Putting it All Together

The `alt-words` function combines all of the functions you've seen so far. It takes in a "word", a dictionary, and a maximum number of edits allowed. _(WARNING: depending on your dictionary, edits of 3 or more can grind your computer to a halt!)_

@n Evaluate `alt-words("maple", WORDS-S, 2)`, and record the first 3 results. In the last column, write what edit(s) were needed to go from "maple" to that word.
