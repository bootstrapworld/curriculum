Another huge job AI does is *similarity*: given an input, find the things most _like_ it. Every recommendation system -- Spotify suggesting songs, Google finding matching images, a dating app proposing matches -- is really a similarity engine.

@vspace{1ex}

The catch is that a computer doesn't _see_ an image or _hear_ a song. Where we see a mountain, the computer only sees a grid of @vocab{pixels} -- numbers. So the first step is always the same:

@vspace{1ex}

@center{*Almost anything can be described by a list of numeric attributes -- and once it is, we can plot it as a __point in space__.*}

@vspace{1ex}

Once every document is a point, "similar" simply means "close together." But there's more than one way to measure "close"!

@vspace{1ex}

Converting documents into a table of points isn't _learning_: every document still has its own row, and a search just compares the query against _all_ of them. That's a real and widely-used AI technique, but it's closer to a *lookup* that it is to *learning* -- the computer is _memorizing_ examples, not learning a general pattern from them.

== Centroids: Supervised Learning
@right{@image{../images/centroid-2d.png}} Labels unlock a powerful idea. If every image with a sun is a point in space, then "sun-ness" is just the point _in the middle_ of those points. +
{empty} +
This is the moment we finally *build a model* -- and where real _learning_ happens. A centroid throws away the individual points and keeps only their summary, *aggregating many documents into one and losing the original detail.* That is exactly what @vocab{training} means: a centroid _generalizes_ a whole group into a single point, instead of memorizing every example.

@vspace{1ex}

*Searching by nearest neighbor _memorizes_ the data; building a centroid _generalizes_ it into a model.*

A centroid lets us reason about a whole *group* instead of a single document, which solves two big limitations of an unlabeled system.

- *Search by concept:* build the centroid of every song tagged "hip-hop," then find unlabeled songs close to it -- so we can search by the _word_ "hip-hop," not just by one example song.
- *Recommendation:* build a centroid for _liked_ images, then find unlabeled images close to it -- so we can search for recommendations based on all those songs, not just by one.


== The Hidden Cost of Supervised Learning

Supervised learning only works because *humans* did the labeling -- and that labor is often invisible. Every CAPTCHA you solve ("click all the traffic lights"), every paid data-labeler, and every content moderator is helping train someone's model. It's worth asking who does this work, and at what cost.
