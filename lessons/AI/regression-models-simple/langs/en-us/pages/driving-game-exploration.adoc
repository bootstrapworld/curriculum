== Playing the Game

@n *Test run:* to get a feel for the simulator, evaluate `train-bev(30)` to run the simulator at a speedy 30 frames per second. Use the left- and right-arrow keys to steer the car once around the track and cross the finish line. In the top-left corner of the simulator, you'll find the current `steering-angle`, along with some some other data. _We won't be saving this data, so don't worry about driving perfectly right now!_

== Examining the Training Data

Each row in your table represents one snapshot of your drive, showing the car's state at that instant.

[.data-table, cols="^1a,^3a", options="header", stripes="none", frame="none"]
|===
| Column       | What it measures
| `speed`      | How fast the car is moving
| `curve`      | How sharply the road is curving (positive = right, negative = left)
| `skew`       | How far out of alignment the car's orientation is from the direction the road is headed
| `offset`     | How far the car is from the center of the lane
|===

== The Challenge of Training

@n What might happen if a self-driving car traveled on a _curved_ road, after being trained on data collected on a perfectly _straight_ road? @fitb{}{@ifsoln{The model would have no idea what to do —}} +
@fitb{}{@ifsoln{ it's never seen a curve. It would predict nearly-zero steering even when the road bends sharply.}}

@n Would you trust a model trained only on `p-data` to drive safely on all roads? @fitb{}{} +
@fitb{}{@ifsoln{Probably not — we haven't seen enough variety. A model can only generalize from what it's been trained on.}}

@n Describe your *ideal* training dataset: how many rows does it have? Is it trained on different tracks? Speeds? What else? @fitb{}{} +
@blanklines{2}{@ifsoln{Hundreds if not thousands of rows, over many different tracks driven in different ways (always on one side of the line or the other, or in the center, different speeds, etc.)}}
