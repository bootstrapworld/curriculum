@n In your own words, explain what `row-n` does. @fitb{}{@ifsoln{It takes a row of the table}}

@center{
@fitbruby{10em}{@ifsoln{row-n}}{function name} {two-colons}

[.callout]
*Code like this (`row2["word"]` or `row2["edit-distance"]`) is called a @vocab{row accessor}.*

@n In your own words, what do row accessors do? @fitb{}{}
@blanklines{2}{@ifsoln{Given a row and the title of a column, they try to access the value stored in that cell of the table.}}
