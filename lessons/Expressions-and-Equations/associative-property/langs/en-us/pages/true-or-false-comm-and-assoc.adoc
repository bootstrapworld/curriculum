Decide whether or not the two expressions are equivalent. Explain your reasoning.

[.FillVerticalSpace, cols="^.^1a, ^.^5a, ^.^1a, ^.^5a, ^.^10a", grid="none", options="header", ]
|===
|	 | Circle 1 							| 	| Circle 2								
| True or False? Justify.

| @n |@show{(coe '(* (* 3 2) 10))}			| = |  @show{(coe '(* (* 2 3) 10))}  
| @ifsoln{True. The Associative Property holds when multiplying.}

| @n |@show{(coe '(+ 6 (* 7 8)))}			| = | @show{(coe '(+ (* 6 7) 8))}   
| @ifsoln{False. The Associative Property holds for multiplication and addition. When we use both operations in one expression, we cannot change groupings.}

| @n |@show{(coe '(* (* 4 5) 2))}			| = | @show{(coe '(* 4 (* 2 5)))}   
| @ifsoln{True. The order and the groupings have changed, but the result remains the same due to the Commutative and Associative Properties.}

| @n |@show{(coe '(* 21 (* 22 23)))}		| = |  @show{(coe '(* (* 21 22) 23))}
| @ifsoln{True. The Associative Property allows changing the groupings in a multiplication expression.}

| @n |@show{(coe '(/ (+ 16 17) 5))}			| = |  @show{(coe '(/ (+ 17 16) 5))} 
| @ifsoln{True. The Commutative Property allows us to change the order of the addends.}

| @n |@show{(coe '(/ (+ 24 6) 10))}			| = |  @show{(coe '(/ 10 (+ 24 6)))} 
| @ifsoln{False. The Commutative Property does not hold for division.}
|===
