Decide whether or not the two expressions are equivalent. Explain your reasoning.

[.FillVerticalSpace, cols="^.^1a, ^.^5a, ^.^1a, ^.^5a, ^.^10a", grid="none", options="header", ]
|===
|	 | Circle 1 							| 	| Circle 2								
| True or False? Justify.

| @n | @show{(coe '(* (* 8 9) 10))}			| = | @show{(coe '(* 8 (* 9 10)))}
| @ifsoln{True. The groupings have changed in an expression with only multiplication.}

| @n |@show{(coe '(- 5 (- 7 8)))}			| = | @show{(coe '(- (- 5 7) 8))}
|@ifsoln{False. The Associative Property does not apply for subtraction.}

| @n |@show{(coe '(+ (+ (+ 78 15) 10) 6))}	| = | @show{(coe '(+ (+ 78 15) (+ 10 6)))}
| @ifsoln{True. The groupings have changed in an expression with only addition.}

| @n |@show{(coe '(- 41 (* 32 63)))} 		| = |  @show{(coe '(- (* 41 32) 63))}
| @ifsoln{False. Other than subtraction (which is not Associative) this expression only includes one set of numbers being mutliplied, so there isn't anything that can be regrouped.}

| @n |@show{(coe '(/ (* (* 8 9) 10) 6))}	| = |  @show{(coe '(/ (* 8 (* 9 10)) 6 ))}
| @ifsoln{True. Both expressions divide the produce of 3 numbers by 6. The Associative Property allows us to multiply those 3 numbers using any groupings we'd like.}

| @n |@show{(coe '(- (+ (+ 4 19) 30) 2))}	| = |  @show{(coe '(+ (+ 4 19) (- 30 2)))}
| @ifsoln{False. The original expression adds 3 numbers before subtracting. We could have used the Associative Property to regroup the addition portion of the expression, but it does not allow us to change which quantities are being subtracted.}
