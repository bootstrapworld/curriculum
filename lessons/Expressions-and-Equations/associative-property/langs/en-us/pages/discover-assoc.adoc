*The Associative Property is true for any operation where _all groupings_ of the numbers produce the same result.*

== Do the Order of the Groupings matter for Addition? (or "Is Addition Associative?")

@n Niko thinks that addition is associative and draws the Circles of Evaluation below as proof. Translate them into expressions and evaluate them. 

[cols="^.^3,^.^3"]
|===
| @show{(coe '(+ (+ 10 8) 3))}
| @show{(coe  '(+ 10 (+ 8 3)))}
| @math{(10 + 8) + 3 =} @fitb{5em}{@ifsoln{21}}
| @fitb{28em}{@ifsoln{@math{10 + (8 + 3) = 21}}}
|===

Complete this second table by drawing another Circle of Evaluation with different groupings, translating both Circles of Evaluation into expressions and evaluating them. Then decide:  *Does the Associative Property hold for problems involving addition*?    @ifsoln-choice{Yes}    No

[.FillVerticalSpace, cols="^.^3,^.^3"]
|===
| @show{(coe '(+ (+ 14 9) 6))}
| @showsoln{(coe  '(+ 14 (+ 9 6)))}
| @fitb{28em}{@ifsoln{@math{(14 + 9) + 6 = 29}}}
| @fitb{28em}{@ifsoln{@math{14 + (9 + 6) = 29}}}
|===

== Is Subtraction Associative?

@n Jacques thinks that subtraction is associative and draws the Circles of Evaluation below as proof. Translate them into expressions and evaluate them. 

[cols="^.^3,^.^3"]
|===
| @show{(coe '(- (- 9 5) 0))}
| @show{(coe  '(- 9 (- 5 0)))}
| @fitb{28em}{@ifsoln{@math{(9 - 5) - 0 = 4}}}
| @fitb{28em}{@ifsoln{@math{9 - (5 - 0) = 4}}
|===

Complete this second table by drawing another Circle of Evaluation with different groupings, translating both Circles of Evaluation into expressions and evaluating them.  Then decide:   *Does the Associative Property hold for problems involving subtraction*?    Yes    @ifsoln-choice{No}

[.FillVerticalSpace, cols="^.^3,^.^3"]
|===
| @show{(coe '(- (- 30 2) 1))}
| @showsoln{(coe  '(- 30 (- 2 1)))}
| @fitb{28em}{@ifsoln{@math{(30 - 2) - 1 = 27}}}
| @fitb{28em}{@ifsoln{@math{30 - (2 - 1) = 29}}}
|===
