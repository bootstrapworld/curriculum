*The Associative Property is true for any operation where _all groupings_ of the numbers produce the same result.*

== Do the Order of the Groupings matter for Addition? (or "Is Addition Associative?")

@n Niko thinks that addition is associative and draws the Circles of Evaluation below as proof. Translate the Circles of Evaluation into expressions and compute their values. 

[cols="^.^3,^.^3"]
|===
| @show{(coe '(+ (+ 10 8) 3))}
| @show{(coe  '(+ 10 (+ 8 3)))}
| @fitb{28em}{@ifsoln{@math{(10 + 8) + 3 = 21}}}
| @fitb{28em}{@ifsoln{@math{10 + (8 + 3) = 21}}}
|===

@vspace{1ex}

Can you think of any addition problems where groupings matters? Write at least three more examples and see if they evaluate to the same thing. _(We've provided the start of one example.)_

[cols="^1a,^1a", options="header"]
|===
| Grouping 1
| Grouping 2

|@math{(14 + 9) + 6 =}@fitb{3em}{@ifsoln{29}}  
|@ifsoln{@math{14 + (9 + 6) = 29}}

|| @vspace{1ex}

|| @vspace{1ex}
|===

@vspace{1ex}

Then decide:  *Does the Associative Property hold for problems involving addition*?    @ifsoln-choice{Yes}    No
