Decide whether or not the two expressions are equivalent. Explain your reasoning. When applicable, provide the property that confirms the equivalence. The first one is done for you.

[.FillVerticalSpace, cols="^.^1a, ^.^5a, ^.^1a, ^.^5a, ^.^5a", grid="none", options="header", ]
|===
|	 | Expression 1 					 		| | Expression 2						| True or False? Justify.
| @n | @show{(coe '(negate (* 5 k)))}    		|=| @show{(coe '(negate (* k 5)))}		| True. The Commutative Property of Multiplication is being applied.
@math{-5k = -5k}}

| @n | @show{(coe '(negate (negate q)))} 		|=| @math{ -q}							| @ifsoln{False. @math{q ≠ -q}}

| @n | @show{(coe '(+ (negate h) 6))}	 		|=| @show{(coe '(+ 6 -h))}				| @ifsoln{True. The Commutative Property is being applied. @math{-h + 6 = 6 + -h}}

| @n | @show{(coe '(* (negate -m) (+ 20 3)))}	|=| @show{(coe '(+ (* m 20) (* m 3)))} 	| @ifsoln{True. The Distributive Property is being applied. @math{m = m}}

| @n | @show{(coe '(negate(negate -y)))}		|=| @math{-y}							| @ifsoln{True. @math{-y = -y}}

| @n | @show{(coe '(+ (negate -g) 0))}			|=| @show{(coe '(- 0 (negate -g)))}		| @ifsoln{False. @math{g + 0 ≠ 0 - g}}

| @n | @show{(coe '(negate f))}					|=|	@show{(coe '(* (negate f) 1))}		| @ifsoln{True. @math{-f = -f}}
