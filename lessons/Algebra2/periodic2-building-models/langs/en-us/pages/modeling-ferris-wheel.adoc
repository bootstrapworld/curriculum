== Modeling with @math{sin}

@link-instructions{Open the Desmos File *Exploring Periodic Functions* to Slide 2: *Modeling the Ferris Wheel Dataset (sine)*.} +
_The Periodic Model used on this slide focuses on the @vocab{frequency} instead of the @vocab{Period}!_ 

@n Because the @vocab{peaks} are at @fitbruby{4em}{@ifsoln{205}}{feet} and the @vocab{valleys} are at @fitbruby{4em}{@ifsoln{5}}{feet}, 
@hspace{2em} we know 
@hspace{2em} @vocab{midline}(@math{k}): @fitbruby{4em}{@ifsoln{105}}{feet} 
@hspace{2em} @vocab{amplitude}(@math{a}): @fitbruby{4em}{@ifsoln{100}}{feet}

@vspace{1ex}

@hspace{1em}_Adjust the sliders for @math{a} and @math{k} accordingly._ 

@n Using the sliders to find the @vocab{frequency}(@math{b}) to the nearest thousandth: @fitbruby{5em}{@ifsoln{~ 0.209}}{cycles per minute} @hspace{2em} and the @vocab{horizontal shift} (@math{h}): @fitb{5em}{@ifsoln{~7.46}}.

@n We know that the @vocab{period}(@math{P}) of the data is 30 minutes and @math{b = \frac{2\pi}{P}}. Does the frequency you found make sense? How do you know? 

@blanklines{1}{@ifsoln{yes. we can estimate that @math{b = \frac{2\pi}{P}} &#8776; @math{\frac{2 * 3}{30}} &#8776; @math{0.2}}}

@n Define the model you found in Function and Pyret notation.

|===
| *Function Notation*
|
@hspace{2em}
@math{f(x) =} @fitbruby{70pt}{@ifsoln{100}}{amplitude} @math{\times}
@math{sin}(
 @fitbruby{70pt}{@ifsoln{0.209}}{frequency} (x - @fitbruby{70pt}{@ifsoln{@math{7.46}}}{horizontal shift})

== Translating from @math{sin} to @math{cos}

@link-instructions{For this section, advance to *Slide 3: "Translating from sine to cosine"* of the *Exploring Periodic Functions* Desmos File.} 

@n Use the definition of @math{p} to complete row 1 below. Then adjust the sliders so that @math{q} perfectly overlaps @math{p}. Record its model settings in row 2.


[cols="1a,5a, 1a,1a,1a,1a", options="header", stripes="none"]
|===
|function 	|definition 							|a 				|b 				|k 				|h 				
|p 			|@math{p(x) = 10(sin(1(x-0)) + 2} 		|@ifsoln{10} 	|@ifsoln{1}  	|@ifsoln{2}  	|@ifsoln{0} 	
|q 			|@math{q(x) = 10(cos(1(x-~1.571)) + 2}  |@ifsoln{10} 	|@ifsoln{1}  	|@ifsoln{2}  	|@ifsoln{~1.571}
|===


@n How are the @vocab{sine} and @vocab{cosine} definitions similar? @fitb{}{@ifsoln{@math{a}, @math{b}, and @math{k} are the same.}}

@n How are the @vocab{sine} and @vocab{cosine} definitions different? @fitb{}{@ifsoln{There was a horizontal shift of @math{\frac{\pi}{2}}}}

@n What is the period of `p`? @fitb{3em}{@ifsoln{@math{2\pi}}} What is the horizontal shift of `q` in terms of @math{\pi}? @fitb{3em}{@ifsoln{@math{\frac{\pi}{2}}}} How does it relate to the period? @fitb{}{It's 1/4 of the period.}
